# Overview

The Development Lab strives to be a holistic community and "one-stop
shop" that incorporates access to 4 main pillars: high-end,
sport-specific training, speed/agility training, tutoring services, and
a central focus on social-emotional development through sports
psychological therapy. The youth sports industry is currently valued at
roughly \$40 billion today, and is projected to reach \$70 billion by
the year 2030. The goal is to create something that is scalable, and can
be used across all different sports and geographic areas.

# Goals

1.  Create a platform that can provide individualized services (i.e.
    training only, tutoring only, etc.), or a comprehensive package that
    includes access to all services on a monthly basis.

2.  Connect families, youth programs, school programs, or club programs
    with a list of providers and services that best meet their overall
    needs and budget.

# Problems We Are Looking to Solve

1.  Numerous providers in saturated markets make it hard for
    trainers/tutors to get continued business, or they have to waste
    resources trying to market for new clients.

    a.  We will bring customers/clients to providers through our
        matchmaking algorithm.

2.  Families have to rely on word of mouth, research, and networking to
    find the best resources and specialists for their children.

    a.  We will create a "one-stop shop" that mitigates stress and saves
        families time to find the resources that they need.

As both an educator and someone deeply connected to youth development,

We're currently speaking with a select group of highly respected

- what challenges professionals are facing,

- how families are currently finding support,

- and how a trusted developmental network could better serve both
  athletes and providers.

Your background and reputation made me feel you'd be incredibly valuable
to speak with.

This is very much still in the early relationship-building and research
phase, and I'd genuinely love to hear your perspective and experiences.

Would you be open to a quick 15--20 minute conversation sometime over
the next couple weeks?

Thanks so much,

Tim Rau\
Founder, The Development Lab

## Conversational Flow

- Predicated on building trust...we are here to build relationships and
  that's how we will succeed.

### Step 1

- Listen to their story, learn more about why they do what they do and
  see if it fits our mission and values

### Step 2

- Understand Pain Points - great leading questions:

  - How do families usually find you?

  - What challenges do you face growing your business?

  - What frustrations do you experience operationally?

  - What do you wish families better understood?

### Step 3

- Validate our vision and introduce our mission and core values

### Step 4

- Find that Collaborative Buy-In - express to specialists that we are
  looking to build something that actually supports professionals and
  families in a way that is symbiotically beneficial for everyone

### Step 5

- Sign, Seal, Deliver - keep it light to keep guard down

  - Are you potentially interested in staying involved as we continue to
    shape this pilot and grow?
